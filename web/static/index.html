Matrix Watcher is an autonomous anomaly detection system that monitors multiple independent data streams in real-time, searching for correlations that emerge from chaos. We observe patterns without attempting to explain them - letting the data speak for itself.

üîç What We're Looking For

We search for correlations across seemingly unrelated domains. When multiple independent sensors show anomalous behavior simultaneously, it suggests something interesting is happening beneath the surface of observable reality.

  • Level 1: Single-source anomalies (recorded, not displayed)
  • Level 2: Two-source correlations (recorded, not displayed)
  • Level 3+: Multiple correlations (displayed & alerted)

üß† Pattern Recognition

Our system learns from historical data to predict future events. When specific conditions occur, we calculate the probability of subsequent events based on past observations:

  • 72-hour lookback window for pattern matching
  • Bayesian probability updates with each observation
  • Automatic calibration based on prediction accuracy
  • Focus on crypto market movements (pumps, dumps, volatility)

‚öôÔ∏è Calibration & Accuracy

The system continuously calibrates itself to maintain prediction accuracy:

  • Threshold Calibration: Adjusts sensitivity based on false positive rates
  • Brier Score Tracking: Measures prediction accuracy (lower is better)
  • Automatic Retraining: Updates models as new data arrives
  • Target: 90%+ well-calibrated predictions

üö´ No AI. Pure Statistics.

Matrix Watcher intentionally does not use artificial intelligence, neural networks, or language models. All predictions are based on pure statistical observation:

  • probability = occurrences / observations
  • Brier score calibration for accuracy validation
  • No hallucinations. No speculation. Just data.

üé® Philosophy

Matrix Watcher doesn't claim to understand why correlations occur. We simply detect them, measure them, and track their predictive power. The universe is full of patterns - some meaningful, some coincidental. Our job is to watch.
